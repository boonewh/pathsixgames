The Rebirth of the Man's Promise

April 27, 2024

With the fall of Plugg and Scourge, our heroes found themselves the new captains of the merchant ship, the Man's Promise. The crew, grateful for their liberation from tyranny, swore their unwavering loyalty to the group. After a brief but profound discussion, Kasmira de la Torre was anointed as Captain, Red as First Mate, Finn as Master-of-Arms, and Varen as the Alchemist and Ship Mage. Their new roles cemented, the group turned to Kroop, the most knowledgeable in pirate ways aboard the ship, for counsel.

Kroop advised them to take the Man’s Promise to Rickety’s Squibs for "squibbing," a process that would alter the ship's masts and rigging, giving it a new, unrecognizable profile. With determination and anticipation, they set sail for the hidden haven of Rickety’s Squibs. Upon arrival, they received a signal from the watchtower, granting them passage into the estuary. There, they were greeted by Rickety Hake and his men. Hake, with a keen eye, assessed the Man's Promise and named his price. Eager to see their ship transformed, the crew agreed, and the Man's Promise was towed into dry dock to begin its metamorphosis.

As work commenced, the crew mingled with the off-duty workers, sharing tales of infamy and plunder. The locals, charmed by the stories, invited the crew to games of ninepins and rounds of beer. It was during one such event that disaster struck. A juvenile water naga, driven mad by the drought conditions, attacked, pulling a local into the river's depths. Without hesitation, Finn and Varen dove in, followed by Red, who cast a bubble spell on himself. Confronting the enraged naga, they discovered her home had been devastated by the drought. With swift and coordinated efforts, they dispatched the naga and rescued the local, earning the profound gratitude of Rickety and the island's inhabitants.

The oppressive heat weighed heavily on everyone, and on the fourth day of their stay, a swarm of giant wasps, displaced by the weather and drawn to the noise of construction, descended upon the village. The heroes sprang into action, battling the horse-sized insects and saving many lives. Amidst the chaos, a Chelish galleon slipped into the estuary unnoticed, anchoring nearby and dispatching a boat towards the shore. Panic ensued, as many feared an attack by the Chelish navy. However, it soon became clear that this was another group of pirates seeking to have their ship squibbed.

The tension was palpable as the captain of the galleon, Free Captain Merrill Pegsworthy, realized his blunder—intruding on the secret operations of Rickety’s Squibs. Preparing for a confrontation, he and his crew readied themselves for a fight. But Kasmira, with calm authority, stepped forward, reassuring Captain Pegsworthy that they meant no harm. Grateful for the reprieve, Pegsworthy asked if he could christen the newly transformed ship. Knowing it was considered good luck for a ship to be christened by a Free Captain, the crew readily agreed, easing the tensions and bringing a sense of camaraderie to the gathering.

However, Rickety Hake remained troubled. His lookout had failed to stop the galleon, and Pegsworthy confirmed no flag had been flown. Rickety requested the heroes to check on his man, fearing something amiss. Agreeing to help, they embarked on a trek through treacherous terrain. Upon reaching the lookout's post, they found a grim scene—the lookout had been attacked by wasps and succumbed to a severe allergic reaction. With heavy hearts, they returned to Rickety, bearing the sad news.

The Man's Promise was on the brink of a new chapter, its crew forged in the fires of battle and bound by loyalty and honor. As they prepared to sail under their new Captain, the specter of future adventures loomed large on the horizon, promising both peril and glory on the high seas.

The Rechristening

May 11, 2024

The Man's Promise had been reborn. Now, rechristened as Scourge's Bane, the heroes gathered over a celebratory meal and drinks to commemorate their triumph over tyranny. The name, chosen to honor their victory over Plugg and Scourge, was a symbol of their new beginning. Rickety Hake, with a flourish, handed a bottle of Sargavan wine to Captain Pegsworthy for the christening ceremony. As the bottle shattered against the keel, Pegsworthy offered a toast: "Good fortune and sure sail await what one can crack the Tidewater Rock." The traditional toast, referring to a small but strategic castle overlooking shipping lanes, ignited a spark of ambition in the heroes. Cracking the rock would mark their rise in the Shackles, elevating them from mere prey to respected Free Captains.

With spirits high, the crew of Scourge's Bane set sail from Rickety's, their heads held high with newfound purpose. However, trouble lurked beneath the waves. On the very first night, as four crew members stood watch, a group of sahuagin, sea-dwelling humanoids often derisively called "fishmen," stealthily climbed the sides of the ship. An observant watcher sounded the alarm just in time, bringing the heroes to the deck to confront the attackers. The battle was fierce but swift, with the heroes dispatching most of the sahuagin and driving the survivors back into the sea. This foreboding start tested their resolve, but they emerged stronger, ready to face the challenges ahead.

Days passed, and the crew's spirits lifted again when a cry of "Ship Ahoy!" rang from the crow's nest. A merchant barque out of Absalom appeared on the horizon, presenting their first true test. The Bane turned hard about, setting in pursuit. The merchant ship attempted to flee, but Kasmira's skillful navigation brought them within range. A fierce exchange ensued, with the merchant ship's catapult shot glancing off the Bane's hull. Finn led the crew in returning fire, using his telekinetic powers to target the opposing catapult crew. As the range closed, Varen took a shot at the merchant ship's first mate, felling him and causing the ship to veer hard to port. With grappling ropes lashing the ships together, the crew of the Bane boarded the merchant vessel with the ferocity of seasoned pirates.

Kasmira, Finn, Jack Scrimshaw, and Samms Toppin led the charge, overwhelming the Absalom marines with swift, decisive strikes. The merchant captain, seeing the inevitable and unwilling to let his crew suffer needlessly, surrendered. Scourge's Bane had claimed its first bounty. Determined not to follow in the footsteps of tyranny, the heroes offered a generous deal to the defeated crew: sail with them to a nearby port to sell the captured ship, and in return, they could choose to join the pirate crew or go free with a small payment from the plunder.

Filling the hold of the Bane with the merchant's goods, they split the crews and set sail for Senghor, a port known for its leniency towards piracy. Red captained the captured merchant ship, while Kasmira remained aboard Scourge's Bane, following closely. Upon reaching Senghor, the crew lived up to their word, taking on a few new members and allowing the rest of the defeated crew to depart freely. Newly enriched from their plunder and the sale of the merchant ship, the crew of the Bane set their sights on their next goal: Tidewater Rock.

The promise of cracking the Tidewater Rock loomed large, a beacon of hope and ambition. With their ship and crew strengthened, the heroes were ready to carve their names into the annals of the Shackles, facing whatever perils and adventures awaited them on the open sea.

The Surprise at Sunset

May 18, 2024

After selling their captured ship in the bustling metropolis, the heroes of "The Scourge's Bane" decided it was time to head back to the tower at Tidewater Rock. Their spirits were high as they set sail, their eyes on the prize of establishing their name in the Shackles. However, their journey took an unexpected turn when the lookout in the crow's nest spotted smoke rising to the east. Ever curious and strategic, Kasmira decided to alter their course to investigate.

As they approached, they saw a Chelish cutter engaged in battle with a pirate ship, the pirate vessel now heavily damaged and listing to one side. Kroop, with his experienced eye, noted the position of the sun, now low on the horizon. He suggested that Kasmira could use the sun to their advantage, sailing in with the blinding light at their back to catch the Chelish cutter by surprise. Seizing the opportunity, Kasmira ordered the crew to prepare for an ambush.

The plan worked flawlessly. The Chelish cutter's sailors and marines were caught completely off guard, unaware of the Bane's approach until they were almost upon them. Boarding ropes were swiftly tied off, and the heroes stormed the cutter. Kasmira led the charge with Owlbear and Barefoot Samms by her side. Red and Rosie followed, while Varen stayed on the deck of the Bane, using his rifle to pick off targets with precision.

The crew of Scourge's Bane quickly realized that the Chelish marines were elite soldiers, armed with muskets and ready for a fight. The battle was intense. Kasmira faced the Chelish captain in a fierce one-on-one duel, her flintlock pistols blazing and her cutlass flashing in the dimming light. With a final, decisive strike, she brought the captain down, securing their victory. Varen, meanwhile, found himself targeted by several musket-wielding marines but managed to take one down and wound another before the melee closed in around him.

Red, with his mighty axe, and Finn, with his telekinetic daggers, waded into the fray, their attacks swift and deadly. The heroes fought with relentless determination, cutting through the marines and sailors. The remaining Chelish sailors, seeing their comrades fall and their captain defeated, decided to surrender rather than face certain death.

With the battle won, the heroes turned their attention to the wrecked pirate ship. The defeated sailors, now prisoners, watched as the Bane's crew plundered the remains, adding to their growing wealth. They decided to sail back to Bloodcove to sell their new bounty, their reputation and fortune steadily rising with each victory.

Their return to Bloodcove was met with interest and respect, the tale of their daring surprise attack spreading quickly among the docks. The heroes of "The Scourge's Bane," now seasoned and confident, prepared for their next adventure, ever closer to claiming their place among the Free Captains of the Shackles.

The Heroes of Bloodcove

May 25, 2024

Arriving at Bloodcove, the heroes of Scourge's Bane sold the Chelish cutter for a hefty profit. With their coffers replenished, they turned their attention to fortifying their beloved ship. For a week, the harbor buzzed with activity as the crew upgraded the hull, making it stronger and more resilient for the dangers ahead. As they mingled with the locals, they inquired about slaver ships, eager to stamp out the vile practice that sickened them, especially Varen. Though their efforts yielded no new leads, their reputation as fierce and honorable pirates spread throughout Bloodcove, adding to their growing legend.

Setting sail once more, the Scourge's Bane ventured through the Shackles, island-hopping and exploring various harbors. In one such port, they caught wind of a Chelish slaver ship rumored to frequent the area. Determined to intercept it, they sailed into a secluded cove, ready to lay an ambush. To their shock, instead of the slaver, a massive Chelish man-o-war named the Dominator appeared on the horizon, its formidable silhouette casting a long shadow over the water.

The Dominator was an imposing sight, twice the size of the Scourge's Bane and bristling with weaponry. Realizing they were outmatched, the crew swiftly retreated up the river to hide, but the Dominator sailed into the mouth of the river, blocking the "The Scourge's Bane" into the river. Luckily, the Dominator was only stopping for water, but its presence at the mouth of the river was a threat to the Bane. If marine discovered the smaller ship, it could mean disaster. Kroop, drawing on his years of pirating wisdom, proposed a daring plan: someone needed to sabotage the Dominator's rudder, crippling the giant ship and allowing the Bane to escape.

Varen and Finn, ever the brave and cunning duo, volunteered for the mission. Swimming underwater, they surfaced briefly to cast invisibility spells before stealthily approaching the Dominator. They climbed aboard, slipping through a window into the ship's interior where the rudder ropes were housed. Just as they began cutting the ropes, the door creaked open, revealing the boatswain's mate and another sailor. Varen acted quickly, his musket firing a fatal shot. Finn hurled a vial of alchemist's fire, engulfing the doorway in flames, while Varen added to the inferno with a spell of burning hands. With the rudder rope severed, they dove out the window and into the river below.

His invisibility spell gone from the attack, Varen was struck by a crossbow bolt from a marine on deck. As the Dominator's crew scrambled in alarm, marines rushed to investigate. Kasmira, hearing the commotion, signaled for the Bane to sail down the river. The Dominator unleashed a volley of ballista and catapult fire, striking the Bane several times, but the heroes managed to escape, pulling Varen and Finn back on board with ropes.

Though disappointed at not encountering the slaver, their spirits soon lifted. Just a day later, they spotted a Rahadoumi ship on the horizon and gave chase. As they closed in, the Rahadoumi crew released a monstrous manticore from a deck hatch. The beast launched itself into the sky, targeting Kasmira in the crow's nest and then Red at the helm. On its third pass, it aimed for the catapult crew. Varen, with precision and calm under pressure, managed to wound the creature. Finn, harnessing his telekinetic powers, delivered the killing blow, sending the manticore crashing into the sea.

With the manticore vanquished, Scourge's Bane overtook the Rahadoumi ship. A fierce battle erupted, but the merchant ship, recognizing their fate, quickly surrendered. Another prize was claimed, and the legend of Scourge's Bane grew ever larger, their adventures etched into the annals of the Shackles.

The Hunt for the Deathknell

June 1, 2024

The Scourge’s Bane continued its relentless hunt across the Shackles, the crew's eyes always on the horizon for new adventures and threats. One day, they spotted a fishing trawler riding low in the water, its sails flapping uselessly in the breeze. Approaching cautiously, they noticed a breach in the hull, water steadily pouring in. There were no signs of battle from their vantage point, prompting Varen to dive into the water and swim to the crippled ship. Climbing aboard, he made his way below decks to investigate.

As Varen descended into the hold, he was suddenly ambushed by a group of sahuagin lurking in the shadows. Surprised but quick to react, he retreated up the stairs, using the narrow passage to his advantage. The sahuagin, forced to climb the stairs one at a time, became easy targets. Varen quickly regained his composure, drawing his musket and firing at the ambushers. The rest of the crew, alerted to the danger, swiftly joined the fray, dispatching the sahuagin with ruthless efficiency. The trawler, stripped of its threats, sank into the depths, leaving the crew wondering about the mysteries it held.

Two days later, a mysterious sighting unsettled the crew. As the sun set, a ship appeared against the fiery backdrop, its details obscured by the glare. Just as the sun dipped below the horizon, the mysterious vessel vanished, eluding all efforts to pursue. The crew was left pondering the nature of this ghostly apparition and its uncanny speed.

The following night, a thick fog rolled in, reducing visibility to mere yards. Out of the gloom, the eerie toll of a ship’s bell rang out. Moments later, a rotting, stained ship emerged briefly from the mist. The crew glimpsed its name before it disappeared back into the fog: Deathknell. The infamous ghost ship, captained by the cursed Whalebone Pilk, was said to haunt the Shackles, collecting skulls to sate his undead crew's thirst for vengeance.

On the third night, the sea turned hostile, with high winds and a dark sky heralding an impending storm. The crew lit lamps early, the inky blackness swallowing the last light of day. Suddenly, the ominous tolling of a bell sounded over the waves. A red ship’s lantern flickered in the distance, and the Deathknell loomed out of the darkness, a ghostly specter challenging the living.

Refusing to be intimidated, Kasmira resolved that the Scourge's Bane would be the hunter, not the hunted. She ordered a hard turn toward the Deathknell, but the storm’s turbulent waters resisted, allowing Pilk to gain an advantage. The ghost ship rammed the Bane, and both crews scrambled to grapple. The decks became a chaotic battlefield as undead and living clashed.

Kasmira noticed that Pilk seemed particularly protective of the ship’s bell. Recalling tales that the bell might be the source of Pilk’s undeath, she decided to take action. Leaving Red in command of the Bane, she swung over to the Deathknell's deck. She fired at Pilk, but the undead captain retaliated with his harpoon gun, narrowly missing her as she dodged. Varen, seeing the battle unfolding, joined in, unloading his musket into Pilk. The combined assault brought Pilk to his knees, yet he cackled maniacally as the Deathknell began to sink.

Realizing the danger, Red quickly ordered the crew to cut the grappling lines, fearing that the Bane would be dragged down with the ghost ship. As the crew worked furiously, Kasmira knew that destroying Pilk alone wouldn’t end the threat; the bell had to be destroyed. She fired two shots at it, each hit producing a sharp, resonant clang. Varen, understanding her intent, took aim and fired, shattering the bell with a final shot.

With the bell’s destruction, the Deathknell shuddered and began to disintegrate, the cursed vessel collapsing in on itself. Kasmira, caught in the chaos, was thrown into the choppy sea. Red, having successfully freed the Bane, took the wheel and steered them to safety. The crew quickly pulled Kasmira aboard, saving her from the stormy waters.

The crew of the Scourge's Bane had triumphed over the legendary ghost ship, vanquishing a true menace of the Shackles. Though the ship needed repairs, the crew celebrated their victory, proud of their bravery and cunning. The legend of the Scourge's Bane grew ever greater, their deeds echoing across the seas as they prepared for whatever challenges awaited them next.

Cracking Tidewater Rock

June 8, 2024

With their infamy spreading like wildfire across the Shackles, the crew of the Scourge’s Bane set their sights on a new goal: claiming Tidewater Rock. The Rock was a fortress of strategic importance, and controlling it would cement their status as true Free Captains. However, as with all things in the Shackles, the path was fraught with complications.

On their way to the Rock, they stumbled upon another pirate ship in the midst of plundering a merchant vessel. The pirate captain, spotting the Bane , turned aggressively, warning them away from his prize. Not one to poach, Kasmira skillfully maneuvered the Bane away, signaling her respect for the pirate code. The crew sailed on, making a brief stop in Genzei. This fortuitous detour placed them in the path of an Aspis Consortium Lugger, a ship ripe for the taking.

The Lugger attempted to flee, but its turn was slow and cumbersome. Kasmira expertly closed the distance, bringing the Bane alongside the merchant ship before it could reach full speed. Varen, ever the sharpshooter, began firing at the Lugger's captain. As soon as the ships were abreast, Finn and Red led the charge, leaping over the railings with the crew hot on their heels. The marines on board the Lugger put up a valiant defense, but Finn and Jack quickly dispatched one, while Varen's musket felled another. Red's axe, as always, cleaved through the opposition with deadly precision.

The Aspis captain, witnessing the swift demise of his crew, decided to lay down his weapons. Kasmira, seizing the moment, grabbed him by his coat and put her pistol to his head. "Surrender, or it's your captain!" she shouted, her voice cutting through the chaos. The marines hesitated, the fight seemingly too brief to warrant surrender. But with their captain in peril, they reluctantly laid down their arms. The Scourge’s Bane had captured another prize, adding to their growing legend.

Finally, they arrived at Tidewater Rock. Unlike their previous encounters, they approached peacefully, seeking to negotiate with Lady Smythee, the lady of the keep. Lady Smythee, impressed by their reputation and recognizing an opportunity, proposed a unique deal: a "Shackles Marriage." This marriage, a pragmatic union lasting one year, would grant the crew full control of the Rock. At the end of the year, they could renegotiate or part ways.

After a brief discussion, it was decided that Finn would marry Lady Smythee, thus becoming Lord of Tidewater Rock. With this agreement, they had "cracked the Rock," achieving a significant milestone in their quest for power and prestige. The reputation of the Scourge’s Bane grew even larger, their deeds spreading across the Shackles.

Now established at Tidewater Rock, the crew utilized a powerful magical seeing glass called the "farglass" to monitor nearby shipping lanes. This enchanted device proved invaluable, spotting a Chelish merchant ship on the horizon. The crew quickly launched from the cove, eager to add another prize to their collection. As they closed in, the crew hurled catapult shots, landing several hits. Varen, with deadly accuracy, opened fire with his musket, while Finn unleashed his magic at the merchant captain.

Despite the onslaught, the merchant captain bravely turned to return fire, engaging Varen in a fierce musket duel. As the ships drew alongside, Varen's shots found their mark, bringing down the captain. Meanwhile, the sail master of the merchant ship, seeing his captain fall, turned his attention to Kasmira. As he attacked, Finn moved to protect his captain. Kasmira, with a quick draw, shot the sail master, and Owlbear, loyal and fierce, finished the job with a powerful swing of his club.

With the sail master defeated and the captain down, the crew of the Bane swiftly took control of the merchant ship. This marked their first prize captured under the watchful eye of Tidewater Rock. The crew celebrated their victory, the thrill of success fueling their ambition. The Scourge’s Bane was not just a ship; it was now a force to be reckoned with, a symbol of their indomitable spirit and cunning prowess.

The Defense of Tidewater Rock

June 15, 2024

The crew of the Scourge’s Bane returned to Tidewater Rock, triumphant with their latest prize. But their celebrations were short-lived as they were greeted with troubling news. While they had been away, the Rock had come under attack by sahuagin. The goatherd, who had been tending the flock, was ambushed by the fiends. A guard intervened, sacrificing his life in the process, allowing the goatherd to escape, though he was gravely injured with a sahuagin spear through his leg. Red, with his divine healing powers, immediately set to work. He mended the boy’s broken bone, cured the infection, and applied his magical healing to the wound. Under Red’s care, the goatherd began to recover, resting peacefully as the heroes turned their attention to securing the Rock.

Kasmira responded swiftly, doubling the watch and questioning everyone who had been on the island during the attack. Despite their efforts, no new clues emerged. That evening, as the workers returned to the tower, the newly set watches spotted another sahuagin attack approaching from the sea. This time, the fiends seemed determined to breach the tower itself. Kasmira, Owlbear, and Sandara positioned themselves in front of the main entrance, ready to defend the tower. Finn, using his powers of invisibility, slipped into the shadows while Red charged out to meet the attackers head-on. Finn stealthily advanced alongside Red, and together they cut through the sahuagin ranks with brutal efficiency. Varen phase-stepped to a high vantage point, picking off the attackers with deadly musket fire, adding to the chaos and carnage.

The battle raged, with Kasmira fending off several sahuagin, though she was badly wounded by their vicious claws and bites. Amid the fray, one sahuagin managed to slip past her, rolling through the chaos and making it inside the tower. As it turned to secure the door, Kasmira, despite her injuries, leveled her black powder pistol and fired, dropping the invader before it could do any more damage. The remaining sahuagin were soon dealt with, but the heroes had little time to savor their victory. From the watchtower, they spotted another wave of attackers approaching—a group of unknown pirates, their boats cutting through the water under the cover of a spell-cast fog.

As the fog thickened, obscuring visibility, a sudden lightning bolt tore through the mist, striking the defenders and slamming into the now-closed tower entrance. The air crackled with energy as Sandara quickly retaliated with her own spell, controlling the water around the attackers' boats and trapping them in a watery embrace. She then summoned her own obscuring mist, shrouding the defenders from sight. Meanwhile, Finn swam out to the attackers’ boats, using the cover of the water to begin sabotaging their hulls from below. As he worked, a commotion erupted at the top of Tidewater Tower—two guards screamed as they were thrown from the battlements, their bodies crashing to the ground near the door.

Realizing the tower itself was under attack, Red cast a spell, granting himself the ability to fly, and soared to the top of the tower to confront the invader. Barefoot Samms, never one to hesitate, sprinted inside the tower, racing up the stairs to aid in the defense. Down at the water, Finn’s sabotage efforts bore fruit as one of the pirate boats began to sink, its crew desperately leaping to the second boat. With a surge of telekinetic power, Finn lifted the damaged boat and hurled it at the remaining vessel. The collision sent pirates sprawling, and one was knocked overboard, clutching at the air as he fell.

Back at the tower, Red arrived to find a sorceress, her invisibility dispelled by her attack on the guards. She turned her attention to Red, draining his life force with a touch that healed her own wounds. She then unleashed a barrage of magic missiles, battering Red further. Undeterred, Red healed himself, but the sorceress lowered her arm, and a tattoo of a snake came to life, slithering down to the floor. The snake lunged at Red, but he managed to deflect it just in time. As the sorceress attempted to retreat deeper into the tower, Barefoot Samms arrived and delivered a powerful punch. The snake struck back, biting Samms three times, each bite draining her life force to heal the sorceress. Despite the pain, Samms stood firm, her fists a blur as she and Red struck the sorceress down, ending the threat at the top of the tower.

Outside, the remaining pirates, their numbers thinned and their boats in disarray, finally made landfall. But their momentum had been blunted, and the heroes of the Scourge’s Bane quickly overpowered them. Varen’s musket fire cut through their ranks, and Kasmira, bolstered by Sandara’s spells, walked on water to join the fray, her pistol felling the last of the attackers. As the final pirate fell, the crew caught sight of a pirate ship to the north of the tower. Realizing they had failed, the ship turned and fled, vanishing into the distance.

With the immediate threat neutralized, the crew of the Scourge’s Bane could finally breathe a sigh of relief. Though the battle had been fierce, they had defended Tidewater Rock from both sahuagin and pirate invaders. The Rock stood strong, a testament to their bravery and resolve. But as they repaired the damage and tended to their wounds, the crew knew that the Shackles would never let them rest for long. More challenges awaited on the horizon, and the crew of the Scourge’s Bane would be ready.

The Secrets of Mancatcher Cove

June 22, 2024

After the harrowing battle at Tidewater Rock, the heroes of the Scourge’s Bane were determined to find answers. The pirate ship that had fled northwest after the attack on the Rock left too many questions unanswered. Who were these attackers, and why had they allied with the sahuagin? Their only lead was a single pirate who had surrendered, willing to talk in exchange for his life. But fortune smiled upon them in another way—on the back of the dead sorceress, who had nearly infiltrated the tower, was a tattooed map. This map would be the key to unlocking a mystery that could change their fate forever.

The captive pirate revealed that the sorceress was none other than Isabella “Inkskin” Locke, the infamous captain of the Thresher. The map on Isabella’s back was said to lead to the fabled lost treasure hoard of Cyrus Wolfe, a pirate legend whose riches were the stuff of Shackles lore. According to the prisoner, Isabella had followed the map to a location marked as Mancatcher Cove but had found a tribe of sahuagin instead of treasure. The sahuagin had struck a deal with Isabella, hiring her to help kill the heroes of Tidewater Rock, all in revenge for the death of their prince, who had been among the sahuagin that ambushed Varen aboard the sinking fishing trawler weeks earlier.

The map led to Mancatcher Cove, a remote archipelago of small, nameless islands south of Shark Island. The cove, named for its treacherous reputation, was the largest isle in the archipelago. With no way of knowing which direction the Thresher had fled, the heroes decided to follow the map themselves, hoping to claim Cyrus Wolfe’s treasure before anyone else could.

Arriving at Mancatcher Cove, the crew prepared for the unknown. The heroes took a small ship’s boat into the harbor, leaving the Scourge’s Bane anchored at a safe distance, ready to respond to any danger. As they sailed into the cove, they found themselves surrounded by high, steep cliffs, with thick vines and vegetation stretching across the top like a living canopy. The scene was almost serene, but it quickly turned deadly.

Sharks and sahuagin appeared in the water below, swiftly swimming under the boat to attack the rudder. Without hesitation, Finn dove into the water, ready to confront the threats head-on, while Varen and Kasmira opened fire from the boat, their shots echoing against the cliffs. The battle escalated as vines from above suddenly descended, ensnaring Varen and Kasmira while they were distracted by the sahuagin. Varen managed to phase step out of the vine’s grasp, but Kasmira was yanked 30 feet into the air, dangling above the water. With calm precision, she aimed her pistol and fired at the vine, severing it and dropping into the water below.

As Kasmira and Varen dealt with the vines, Red was pulled a staggering 90 feet into the air by another vine. His mighty axe flashed in the dim light, and Varen’s musket roared as they fought back against the living plant. After a fierce struggle, they finally managed to kill the monstrous vine. Red, using his quick reflexes, grabbed another vine and dropped safely back down into the water.

Meanwhile, Finn was battling the sahuagin and sharks single-handedly, outnumbered and taking a brutal beating. Despite the odds, he fought with tenacity, using his telekinetic powers and weapons to strike back. Even as Red was being hauled into the sky by the vine, he managed to send healing magic to Finn, keeping him in the fight. With Red’s support, Finn turned the tide, dispatching the remaining attackers with a deadly combination of skill and sorcery.

With the sahuagin and vines defeated, the heroes beached their boat and took a moment to catch their breath. But their minds were already turning to the treasure. The map on Isabella’s back, the clues they had gathered—it all led to this moment. They began deciphering the riddles that would guide them to the treasure, their sharp eyes scanning the landscape for the hidden entrance. It wasn’t long before they found what they were looking for: a natural cave that promised to be the key to Cyrus Wolfe’s legendary hoard.

The heroes of the Scourge’s Bane stood at the entrance, the thrill of discovery coursing through them. The path ahead was dark and unknown, but they had faced worse. Together, they would uncover the secrets of Mancatcher Cove and claim the treasure that had eluded so many before them.

The Depths of Mancatcher Cove

June 29, 2024

Venturing deeper into the cave that they believed held the fabled treasure of Cyrus Wolfe, the crew of the Scourge’s Bane stumbled upon a peculiar sight: a tangle of roots clutching a humanoid skull. Above, through a hole in the cave’s ceiling, a gnarled tree trunk loomed, its twisted branches resembling a crown. "The old king," Kasmira whispered, recognizing the clue from the map. Excitedly, the group pulled out their picks and shovels and began digging at the base of the tree.

Instead of a chest filled with gold, they unearthed a wooden barrier. Breaking through, they found not riches but a deep, dark shaft that led further into the earth. Undeterred, the crew prepared their ropes and descended into the abyss. At the bottom, they discovered an underwater network of natural caves. Resigned to the challenge, they drank potions that allowed them to breathe underwater and plunged into the depths, unaware that they were entering the home of the sahuagin.

After swimming 20 feet down and through a natural passage, they emerged in a cavern. Kasmira swam forward to scout the room but was ambushed by a group of sahuagin guards lying in wait. Red, always ready to defend his crew, swam above Kasmira to engage the enemy from above. As he attacked, a shark lunged at Red, while three sahuagin converged on Kasmira. Overwhelmed, she took several hits and slipped into unconsciousness, bleeding into the dark water. Varen, who had magically fortified his musket for underwater combat, began picking off sahuagin from the back, his shots muffled but deadly in the aquatic environment. A few sahuagin broke off to attack Finn, but he held his ground, fighting back fiercely.

The sahuagin focused most of their attacks on Red, but his sturdy armor and swift axe strikes kept him standing. He fended off the blows, cutting down his foes with powerful slashes. Despite the chaos, Red noticed Kasmira drifting, her blood mingling with the seawater. With a final swing, he dispatched the last of his attackers and swam to her side, quickly administering healing magic. The light returned to her eyes as Red’s magic closed her wounds.

With Kasmira back swimming again, the group pressed forward, determined to find the treasure. They surfaced into an air pocket where they discovered a small, dry room, filled with vines and overgrown with small flowers that filled the air with a sweet fragrance. Moving in to investigate, they unknowingly disturbed an intelligent plant. In defense, the plant released a cloud of pollen. Most of the group choked back the pollen’s effects, but Varen succumbed, collapsing into a deep sleep. Red quickly dragged him out of the pollen's reach while Kasmira and Finn destroyed the plant with swift, decisive strikes. The room held little of value, save for a few magical trinkets, so they moved on, determined to find Wolfe’s hidden hoard.

As they navigated the labyrinthine caves, they easily dispatched another group of sahuagin guards, finding a curious clutch of eggs. Varen studied them and realized they belonged not to sahuagin but to a peaceful species known as locathah. Understanding the significance, they left the eggs undisturbed and continued deeper into the caves. In the next chamber, they found a lone female locathah chained to the wall, disfigured from torture. The sahuagin had captured her, forcing her to produce eggs for their nefarious purposes. The crew freed her and ensured she could escape the caves safely.

Venturing further, the water grew cloudy and foul, the signs of a recent feeding frenzy. Ahead, a group of sahuagin tore into their meal with savage hunger. Finn, using the murky water to his advantage, snuck up behind one and killed it silently. Varen fired his musket, the loud report echoing through the water and alerting all within earshot. A group of weaker sahuagin immediately fled, leaving the warriors to face the intruders. Red stood firm, his axe flashing through the water as he cut down foes left and right. Kasmira and Varen fired their guns at the attackers, while Finn’s telekinetic blade slashed through the sahuagin ranks. With the warriors dispatched, the crew interrogated the remaining sahuagin, extracting the location of the tribe's "throne room." Deciding they could not leave this evil behind to fester, they executed every last one.

Guided by the directions given by the sahuagin, the crew moved purposefully toward the throne room. Entering the chamber, they were met by the sight of the sahuagin king, a massive, four-armed monstrosity rising from his throne. "Why do you dare invade my throne room?" he bellowed, his voice echoing through the water. Two concubines, armed with heavy crossbows, stepped out from behind a curtain, ready to defend their king. The crew responded with a question of their own: "Why did you try to assassinate us?" The king’s rage grew palpable as he shouted, "You killed my son!" Without missing a beat, Finn quipped, "Your son was stupid." This insult sent the king over the edge, and the battle began.

Kasmira, always ready to lead the charge, moved first, but the king was faster. He lunged with his trident, stabbing her deeply. The concubines fired their crossbows, but Varen quickly moved to intercept them, shooting one as he closed in. Flanking him, the concubines attempted to overwhelm him, but Red rushed to Varen’s aid, his axe swinging with deadly force. Meanwhile, Finn maneuvered into a position to flank the king, but the sahuagin monarch continued his relentless assault on Kasmira, stabbing her again with his cursed trident. The magic of the weapon began to take its toll, turning her flesh to coral and slowing her movements. Weakened and unable to fend off the attacks, Kasmira collapsed, unconscious once more.

Varen and Red dispatched the concubines and turned their attention to the king. Varen leveled his musket and fired, the bullet finding its mark and finally bringing the sahuagin king to his knees. With the last of the sahuagin defeated, Red brought Kasmira back up once again and the crew searched the room, finally uncovering Wolfe’s long-lost treasure hidden beneath the throne. Ironically, one of the greatest treasures was the chest itself, enchanted to hold far more than its size would suggest. Inside, they found a wealth of spices, gold, furs, silks, and magical artifacts—the fabled bounty of Cyrus Wolfe.

As they gazed upon their newfound riches, a sense of triumph washed over them. The journey to Mancatcher Cove had been fraught with danger and uncertainty, but now they stood victorious, the treasures of a legendary pirate in their grasp. The crew of the Scourge’s Bane had faced down their enemies, solved the mysteries of the cove, and claimed a prize that would make their names echo across the Shackles. They knew this was only the beginning; the seas held many more secrets, and they were ready to face them all.

With their ship waiting above and the promise of new adventures on the horizon, the crew prepared to make their way back to the surface, their spirits high and their hearts filled with anticipation for what lay ahead. As they emerged from the depths, the sunlight breaking through the waves, they couldn't help but smile—victory tasted sweeter with every passing day.

The Final Battle with the Thresher

July 6, 2024

Emerging from the depths of Mancatcher Cove with Wolfe's treasure in hand, the crew of the Scourge’s Bane wasted no time basking in the sunlight. Instead, they slipped stealthily into the water, staying hidden beneath the surface to avoid prying eyes. Kasmira and Red, their lungs still empowered by magic to breathe underwater, submerged beneath the waves alongside Finn and Varen. They suspected that the pirates who had attacked Tidewater Rock might be waiting to ambush them, and they had no intention of being caught off guard. Carefully, they made their way back to the Bane, ready to prepare for the hunt against the attacking pirate ship, the Thresher.

As the sun crested the horizon the next day, their suspicions were confirmed. The Thresher appeared just a mile away, its sails fully unfurled, cutting through the waves with menacing intent. The crew of the Scourge’s Bane recognized the new figure standing on the deck—Knuckles Grype, a burly, scarred man with a ruthless reputation, now acting as captain. The sight of their enemy fueled their determination. Under Kasmira’s command, the crew swiftly set the sails, and the Bane surged forward, ready for the inevitable clash.

The tension mounted as both ships charged headlong at one another, neither captain willing to back down. The distance between them closed rapidly, the wind whipping through their sails as the two vessels hurtled towards a collision. Just before impact, Varen took careful aim with his musket, firing a shot that struck Grype squarely. The pirate captain gritted his teeth against the pain but remained resolute, refusing to give an inch. The two ships collided with a thunderous crash, sending crew members sprawling across the decks as wood splintered and masts creaked under the strain. Both ships lurched to a halt, locked together in a deadly embrace.

As the crews scrambled to their feet, the ships slipped side by side, and chaos erupted as both crews rushed the rails to engage in brutal combat. Finn, ever the opportunist, used his magic to vanish from sight, slipping through the melee like a shadow. He quickly coordinated with Barefoot Samms, laying out a plan to take down Grype. As Samms moved in, Grype managed to land a few powerful blows, but the combined efforts of the invisible Finn and the relentless Samms caught him off guard. Kasmira and Varen added to the onslaught, firing their pistols and musket with deadly precision. Under the concentrated assault, Grype finally succumbed, collapsing onto the deck.

With their captain down, the morale of the Thresher’s crew crumbled. Realizing the battle was lost, they dropped their weapons and surrendered, their defeat sealing the end of the attacks on Tidewater Rock. The crew of the Scourge’s Bane had won the day, avenging their home and securing another victory. Red took command of the captured ship, steering it toward Quent, where they would sell their prize. While in port, they also took the time to mount a magical figurehead, Besmara’s Bones, on the prow of the Bane, a trophy from Wolfe’s treasure.

As they sailed into the bustling port of Quent, stories of their daring exploits spread like wildfire through the taverns and docks, adding to their growing infamy among the pirates of the Shackles. Their reputation had reached such heights that even Tessa Fairwind, the Pirate Lord of Quent, made an appearance at a local tavern to personally toast the crew. She raised her glass, a smile playing on her lips, and spoke of their courage and cunning. In a moment that hinted at future opportunities, Tessa pulled Kasmira aside and suggested they petition the Hurricane King for a Letter of Marque—a document that would officially recognize them as pirates of the Shackles. The crew of the Scourge’s Bane had come a long way, but their journey was far from over. With a new goal in sight, they prepared for the next chapter of their adventure, their spirits high and their ambitions even higher.
